# Target Competitors — Rythma

> Used by alternatives-post, comparison-post, and best-of-list skills. Keep treatment fair and factual.

## Tier 1 — Direct competitors (menopause / perimenopause-specific apps)

- **Balance (Newson Health)** — popular menopause app with symptom tracking and a shareable health report. Clinician-backed brand. Strong on education, broad menopause focus.
- **Caria** — menopause/perimenopause companion app with tracking and AI guidance.
- **Perry** — perimenopause community + tracking app, peer-support focused.
- **Health & Her** — menopause app + supplement brand with a symptom tracker.
- **MenoLabs / Menopod and similar** — menopause-focused trackers, often tied to supplements.

*Rythma's wedge vs. Tier 1:* prediction of difficult days from learned personal patterns, built around the unpredictability of perimenopause specifically (not general menopause education), and a clean doctor report.

## Tier 2 — Adjacent tools (general cycle trackers used by perimenopausal users)

- **Flo** — largest general period & cycle tracker. Strong predictions for regular cycles; not built for perimenopausal irregularity.
- **Clue** — science-forward period tracker, privacy reputation. Cycle-centric.
- **Natural Cycles** — FDA-cleared for contraception; built around regular ovulatory cycles.
- **Stardust** — cycle tracker with a younger, astrology-tinged audience.
- **Apple Health / Cycle Tracking** — built-in iOS logging, minimal perimenopause intelligence.

*Rythma's wedge vs. Tier 2:* these assume a predictable ~28-day cycle and degrade precisely when perimenopause makes cycles erratic — the moment Rythma is designed for.

## Writing guidelines for competitor treatment

- Be accurate and fair. State what each competitor genuinely does well before differentiating.
- Never invent missing features, fake quotes, or fabricated review scores. If unsure of a current feature/price, say "check their site" and link out rather than guess.
- No disparagement or implying a competitor is unsafe. Differentiate on fit ("built for regular cycles vs. built for perimenopausal irregularity"), not on attacks.
- Always disclose that Rythma is our product when we name it in a comparison.
- Prices change — link to the competitor's own pricing/listing instead of quoting numbers.
- Comparisons are conversion content but must read as genuinely helpful to someone choosing an app, or they backfire.
