# Content Strategy — Rythma Blog

## Strategic thesis

Perimenopause is high-anxiety, under-served, and full of unanswered questions — exactly the moment people turn to AI engines and search for explanations. Generic period apps and generic health sites both fail this audience: the apps assume a regular cycle, and the health sites bury answers in clinical hedging. Rythma's blog can own the middle: clear, well-sourced, genuinely useful answers about perimenopause symptoms and how to track them — structured so AI engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini) quote Rythma as the authority. We win by going deep on a tight set of clusters, interlinking aggressively, and pairing every explanation with the practical "and here's how to see your own pattern" angle that leads to a download.

## Internal linking plan

- Cluster 1 hub = **Perimenopause Symptoms Statistics 2026**. Every Cluster 1 post links up to it; it links down to the key explainers.
- Cluster 2 hub = **Best Perimenopause Tracking Apps 2026**.
- Cross-link clusters where natural (e.g. a symptoms post links to "how to track an irregular cycle").
- Each post: link to its hub + 1–3 siblings. Natural anchor text only.

## Publishing calendar (first 4 weeks)

- **Week 1 (Cluster 1 sprint):** Posts 1–4 of Cluster 1 (the four flagship posts in topic-queue.md).
- **Week 2:** Open Cluster 2 — "Best Perimenopause Tracking Apps 2026" (hub) + "Why Period-Tracking Apps Fail in Perimenopause" + 1–2 glossary posts from Cluster 1.
- **Week 3:** Deepen Cluster 1 (sleep, brain fog, hot flashes) + 1 Cluster 2 alternatives post.
- **Week 4:** First Cluster 3 post ("Plan your life around hard days") + Cluster 2 "How to read your symptom patterns" + glossary fill-ins.

Cadence target: ~2 deep posts/week (statistics + alternatives) plus 2–3 fast glossary posts/week.

## Success metrics + kill criteria (90-day checkpoint)

- **Leading signals:** posts indexed; appearance/citation in AI answers for "perimenopause symptoms / tracking" queries; impressions in Search Console; blog → App Store referral clicks.
- **Goal by day 90:** 20–25 posts live; both primary cluster hubs ranking/cited; measurable blog → App Store referral traffic.
- **Kill / pivot criteria:** if after 90 days at cadence there is zero organic or AI-citation traction on the primary clusters, reassess topic targeting (likely too broad or too competitor-crowded) before adding clusters.

## What we're NOT writing

- Individualized medical advice, diagnosis, or dosage guidance.
- General fertility / contraception / pregnancy content (different audience, dilutes focus).
- Broad "women's wellness" lifestyle fluff unconnected to perimenopause.
- Anything we can't source to a primary or clinical authority.
- Competitor hit-pieces — comparisons stay fair and factual (see target-competitors.md).
