## Core Differentiators

- Built **specifically for perimenopause** — not a 28-day-cycle period app bolted onto midlife. It expects irregularity instead of breaking on it.
- **Predicts difficult days** ("hard days") before they arrive by learning the user's personal symptom patterns.
- **Pattern intelligence** — the longer you use it, the better it understands your individual rhythm.
- **Doctor report** — generates a clear, shareable summary of symptoms and patterns to bring to medical appointments.
- Designed around the lived reality of perimenopause: planning, energy, relationships, work — not just logging.

## Key Features

- Symptom tracking tailored to perimenopause (hot flashes, sleep disruption, mood shifts, brain fog, irregular bleeding, and more).
- Personalized prediction of difficult / high-symptom days.
- Pattern detection across an unpredictable cycle.
- Shareable doctor report for appointments.
- Designed for life-planning: see what's likely coming so you can plan around it.

## Target Audience

Women roughly in their 40s and early 50s experiencing perimenopause — irregular cycles, new and confusing symptoms, and a sense that standard period apps no longer fit. They want to understand what their body is doing, anticipate hard days, and feel back in control. Often frustrated by being dismissed or under-informed.

## Style Enforcement

**Banned phrases (AI-tell and vague-authority):**
- "delve", "navigate the complexities", "in today's fast-paced world", "game-changer", "unlock", "elevate", "robust", "leverage" (as a verb), "tapestry", "testament to", "it's important to note", "needless to say"
- Vague authority: "studies show", "research has proven", "experts say", "everyone knows", "doctors agree" — name the source instead.
- Hype/medical overreach: "cure", "fix your hormones", "balance your hormones" (as a promise), "miracle", "revolutionary"

**Punctuation / format:**
- No emoji in post body.
- Use em dashes sparingly.
- Sentence case for most headings; numbers in stat headlines.

**Required style:**
- Warm, clear, non-condescending. Talk to a smart adult who is tired of being dismissed.
- Cite primary/clinical sources (NICE, ACOG, NAMS/The Menopause Society, NHS, peer-reviewed studies, national statistics bodies) over blog roundups.
- Paraphrase data in your own words; never copy a source's phrasing.
